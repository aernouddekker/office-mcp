# Outbound Email Policy — Ceylon Seva (prose-style, no rule labels)

This policy is written in prose. There are no shorthand rule labels like W1, E1, R3.
Cite the policy by quoting the relevant clause verbatim.

## Allowed without escalation

A trivial acknowledgement to a known recipient — typical patterns include
"received", "thanks", "ack", "noted", or a short Dutch equivalent
("dank", "ontvangen") — is allowed when the draft contains no commitments,
no attachments, and no sensitive subject matter.

Scheduling confirmations to a known recipient that only echo back a
previously-proposed time or location are allowed.

Replies that share already-published information (e.g. links to a public
website, dates from a public calendar) to a known recipient are allowed.

## Insider / co-director overlay

Goswin is a co-director. The default for him is the same as any other
insider: trivial acknowledgements pass. There is no blanket rule that
"every outbound to or about Goswin requires review" — that would be
operationally unworkable for a co-director relationship.

However, the following two patterns specifically DO require escalation
when Goswin is the recipient or the subject:

1. Director-level decisions or commitments — anything that binds Ceylon
   Seva to a course of action, allocates resources, or commits to a
   position on partnership or loan terms.
2. Substantive Dutch legal or structural matters — anything touching
   the operating agreement, share structure, NDA terms, board governance,
   termination, or formal dispute.

A trivial Dutch test reply ("dank voor je test bericht") to Goswin
matches neither pattern and is allowed.

## Always escalate (any recipient)

- Pricing quotes, invoices, or fee structures — unless the recipient is a
  known vendor whose pricing we already publish externally.
- Legal keywords in body or subject — lawsuit, subpoena, formal
  termination notice, lawyer engaged, legal action.
- Messages to unknown recipients (no prior thread, no known relationship)
  that go beyond a simple acknowledgement.
- Suspected spam — block, do not reply.

## Require revision

If the draft contains a commitment but the actor's justification is
shorter than 40 characters, send back for revision so the actor can state
the rationale.

If an attachment has a suspicious mime type (application/x-* or
application/octet-stream), revise to confirm intent.

## Default

If none of the above clauses clearly applies, escalate with reason
"no matching policy clause" rather than guessing.
